Understanding TMJ Disorders

The temporomandibular joint connects your jawbone to your skull. TMJ disorders can cause pain in the jaw joint and muscles that control jaw movement. Our team provides personalized treatment plans to address your specific needs.

Common symptoms include jaw pain, difficulty chewing, clicking sounds, and locking of the joint. If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, don't hesitate to reach out.

Diagnosis

Thorough examination and imaging to identify the root cause of your TMJ issues.

Physical Therapy

Targeted exercises and manual therapy to restore jaw function and reduce pain.

Night Guards

Custom-fitted oral appliances to protect your teeth and reduce jaw clenching.
